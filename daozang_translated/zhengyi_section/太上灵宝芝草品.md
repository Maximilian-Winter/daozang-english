# 太上灵宝芝草品

**The Supreme Numinous Treasure: Catalog of Sacred Fungi**

*Scripture Title:* The Supreme Numinous Treasure: Catalog of Sacred Fungi. Author unknown, likely composed during the Northern and Southern Dynasties or the Sui-Tang period. Not recorded in the *Lingbao Scripture Catalog*. One scroll. Source text: *Zhengtong Daoist Canon*, Orthodox Section, Part One.

**The Supreme Numinous Treasure: Catalog of Sacred Fungi**

The art of prolonging life fundamentally relies upon consuming elixirs to attain longevity. As the *Book of Hidden Marvels* states: "The immortals command spirits." Thus, Guangchengzi dwelled upon Mount Kongtong, revealing the Dao and its governance to the Yellow Emperor. Chisongzi ascended the Kunlun Palace, ingesting *shuiyu* (water jade) to instruct the Flame Emperor. They roamed with wind and rain, ascending and descending to investigate subtle omens, traversing famous mountains, and observing flora—unveiling the mysteries of the obscure.

The forms of sacred fungi (*zhi*) are manifold, their myriad manifestations difficult to distinguish. Thus, illustrations were drawn, their appearances recorded, and their descriptions transmitted, that seekers might follow them to attain the path of immortality.

**Azure Jade Fungus** grows upon the mountains of Yangzhou. Its flavor is sweet and sour; consuming it grants twelve myriad years of life. Wang Ziqiao ate it and ascended as an immortal. A green tiger guards it. According to the illustration, it has four layers, with two branches on the second layer—its stem and cap are entirely azure.

**Cinnabar Jade Fungus** (also called *Zishi Fungus*) grows upon the mountains of Yuzhou. Its form is as depicted, and its flavor is bitter. Chisongzi consumed it and attained immortality. A red tiger and red fish guard it. According to the illustration, its stem and cap are crimson, with two layers. The lower layer bears two small fungi, and the upper layer has two small fungi, each with a cap.

**Yellow Jade Fungus** grows upon Mount Penglai. Its color is yellow, and its flavor is pungent. The Eastern King Father consumed it, attaining ninety thousand years of life and immortality. A yellow tiger and yellow fish guard it. It has three layers; the lower layer bears three branches, one of which produces nine caps. Its stem and cap are entirely yellow.

**White Jade Fungus** grows within Mount Fangzhang. Its flavor is pungent and white. It has four layers; on the lowest layer, two grow upward, and three grow upon the upper layer. It may also sprout upon great stones, in yellow sands, at the roots of rotting wood, beneath tall trees, or on the shady sides of famous mountains. Consuming it grants immortality. A white tiger guards it.

**Black Jade Fungus** grows within Mount Gui. Its flavor is sweet and sour. Xianyuzi consumed it and attained immortality. A black tiger guards it nearby, its form as depicted. It has two small branches growing in layers, its color black, with four layers—its stem and cap are both crimson.

**Five Thearchs’ Jade Fungus** grows within the renowned mountains of Songgao. It possesses five colors and five transformations corresponding to the Five Phases. Its flavor changes with the four seasons. Consuming it in accordance with the seasons grants ten thousand years of immortality. It has three layers; the lower two layers bear two opposing branches.

**Azure Thearch’s Jade Fungus** grows beneath great trees in famous mountains, veiled by yellow clouds. Its cap is azure, its lining crimson, its stem white, and its flavor sour. Harvested on *jiayi* days in spring and consumed, it grants ageless immortality.

**Cinnabar Thearch’s Jade Fungus** grows on the sunny sides of famous mountains. Its cap is crimson, its lining yellow, and its stem white. Red clouds hover above it, and its flavor is sweet and bitter. Harvested in summer and consumed, it grants four thousand years of immortality.
