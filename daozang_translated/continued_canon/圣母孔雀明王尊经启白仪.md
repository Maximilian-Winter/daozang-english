# 圣母孔雀明王尊经启白仪

**The Most High Primordial Heavenly Worthy’s Scripture of the Radiant Moonlight Empress, Holy Mother Heavenly Worthy, and Peacock Bright King**

**Scripture Title:** *The Most High Primordial Heavenly Worthy’s Solemn Scripture of the Radiant Moonlight Empress, Holy Mother Heavenly Worthy, and Peacock Bright King*
*Compiler unknown; collated and printed by Zhang Guoxiang of the Ming dynasty. Three fascicles.*
*Source text: Wanli Continuation of the Daoist Canon.*

---

**Invocation of The Most High Primordial Heavenly Worthy’s Scripture of the Radiant Moonlight Empress, Holy Mother Heavenly Worthy, and Peacock Bright King**

*Music begins. Officiants take their posts. The sacred assembly is arrayed. The scripture is opened.*
*Each holds incense and makes invocation according to ritual. The chime sounds.*

**Officiant:**
*Opening the case to expound the profound, Heavenly Worthy—*

**Assembly (in unison):**
*Great Compassionate One, who opens the case to expound the profound, Heavenly Worthy!*

**Opening Chant:**
*From the boundless kalpas, the immortal verses unfold,*
*Yet their origin and end remain untold.*
*Culture long propagated to sustain the world’s course,*
*Prosperity and joy abound, aiding the Primordial Emperor’s force.*
*The Great Teaching endures, unbroken and complete,*
*Its mysteries expounded with structure replete.*
*Immortal and mortal blend, divine resonance flows,*
*This scripture’s radiance shines, forever to grow.*

**Assembly (in unison):**
*Great Compassionate One, who opens the case to expound the profound, Heavenly Worthy!*

*Music concludes. The assembly recites:*
*We bow our heads to the Lord of the Dharma’s pure sound,*
*We take refuge in the Peacock Compassionate Worthy, profound.*
*All Perfected Ones fill the River of Stars,*
*The constellations of the celestial spheres.*
*Star officials of destiny, true lords of the year,*
*Spirits who spread illness and plague far and near,*
*Deities who wield power, bestowing fortune and grace,*
*Guardians of realms and altars, their presence we embrace.*
*Attendants of incense and scripture, their ranks arrayed,*
*At the first strike of the emperor’s bell, their voices pervade.*
*Its sound resounds through the ten directions’ span,*
*As the sacred name is proclaimed, its echo reaches the nine lands.*
*Having received our invocation, may they descend like clouds,*
*Grace the scripture’s assembly, witness our vows and vows’ shrouds.*
*Today, we offer this rite for [name’s] intent,*
*Repeating: [name].*

*Beset by calamity and affliction, we seek refuge in the gate of absolution,*
*To prolong life and blessings, we reverently commence this rite of expiation.*
*May the nine knots and ten entanglements of karmic guilt dissolve like melting ice,*
*May the seven harms and eight adversities scatter like wind-blown mist.*
*When merit is complete, auspicious omens shall descend,*
*The assembly, with utmost devotion, extols the sacred name.*

**Opening Chant:**
*We take refuge in the Peacock, Great Compassionate Worthy,*
*We take refuge in the Heavenly Kings and all Perfected Ones.*
*Dispelling disaster, prolonging life, bestowing fortune,*
*In heaven and earth, the numinous scripture is expounded.*

*The chime sounds:*
*Unsealing the Dragon Palace’s vault,*
*Bearing forth the jade case’s treasure.*
*The Peacock Worthy’s scripture—*
*In harmony, we respond as one.*

*The chime sounds:*
*Disaster-dispelling, life-prolonging Heavenly Worthy.*

**Assembly (in unison):**
*Great Compassionate One, disaster-dispelling, life-prolonging Heavenly Worthy!*

**Opening Chant:**
*Great indeed! The Primordial One expounds the immortal child,*
*From boundless kalpas past, the Peacock King’s tale is styled.*
*Guiding goodness, cultivating truth with depth untold,*
*Vastly opening expedient means, the numinous altar unfolds.*

*The chime sounds:*
*Great Compassionate One, disaster-dispelling, life-prolonging Heavenly Worthy.*

**Opening Chant:**
*For all who recite this scripture, first chant the incantation of body, mouth, and mind,*
*Proceeding to the Mysterious Essence Mantra of the scripture’s opening.*
*Bow deeply, kneel, or sit upright at the altar’s edge,*
*Recite with reverence the invocation of homage.*

**Assembly recites:**
*We gaze upward to the Three Pure Realms,*
*The supreme Lords of the Three Realms’ height.*
*We gaze upward to the Great Canopy Heaven,*
*The supreme Sovereign of the Mysterious Vault.*
*We gaze upward to the Three Pure Realms’ bounds,*
*The Perfected Ones attending the Celestial Throne.*
*The wondrous Dao is beyond thought,*
*Yet through it, we enter the gate of the profound.*
*The wondrous scripture reveals the mysterious essence,*
*Guiding the lost in the dust to their proper ends.*
*We now purify our three karmas,*
*Reciting with mouth and holding with mind.*
*We pray, O Great Compassionate One,*
*Receive our humble plea.*
*May your compassionate light illumine our lowly state,*
*Your wisdom hearing our voices’ plea.*
*As we chant and recite,*
*May the sound resound through the ten directions’ space.*
*May all our vows be fulfilled,*
*As the scripture declares.*
*Henceforth, may we advance in cultivation,*
*Attaining the Dao and serving the Perfected Ones.*

*With devoted hearts, we bow in homage and invoke—*
*Invoking again and again.*
