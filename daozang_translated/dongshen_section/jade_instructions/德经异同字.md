# 德经异同字

**Variants in the *Dejing*: Graphical Discrepancies**

**Text Title:** *Variants in the *Dejing*: Graphical Discrepancies*
**Author:** Anonymous (Tang Dynasty)
**Volume:** One
**Source Edition:** *Daoist Canon of the Zhengtong Era*, *Dongshen* Section, *Jade Instructions* Category

**Variants in the *Dejing*: Graphical Discrepancies**
**Chapters 1–2**
"非乎" (fēi hū): 作 "非" (fēi)
"故致数舆无舆" (gù zhì shù yú wú yú): 作 "故致数誉无誉" (gù zhì shù yù wú yù)

**Chapters 3–4**
"勤而行之" (qín ér xíng zhī): 作 "勤能行" (qín néng xíng)
"大笑之" (dà xiào zhī): 作 "大笑之" (dà xiào zhī)
"道若颣" (dào ruò lèi): 作 "道若类" (dào ruò lèi)
"若谷" (ruò gǔ): 作 "若俗" (ruò sú)

**Chapter 5**
"民之所恶" (mín zhī suǒ wù): 作 "人之所恶" (rén zhī suǒ wù)
"王公以为称" (wáng gōng yǐ wéi chēng): 作 "王公以为名" (wáng gōng yǐ wéi míng)

**Chapter 6**
"吾是以知" (wú shì yǐ zhī): 作 "是以知" (shì yǐ zhī)

**Chapters 7–8**
"大盈" (dà yíng): 作 "大满" (dà mǎn)
"天下正" (tiān xià zhèng): 作 "天下政" (tiān xià zhèng)

**Chapter 9**
"莫大於欲得" (mò dà yú yù dé): 作 "莫甚於欲得" (mò shèn yú yù dé)

**Chapters 10–12**
"德善" (dé shàn): 作 "得善" (dé shàn)
"孩之" (hái zhī): 作 "忄亥之" (hài zhī)

**Chapter 13**
"投其角" (tóu qí jiǎo): 作 "驻其角" (zhù qí jiǎo)
"措其爪" (cuò qí zhuǎ): 作 "错其爪" (cuò qí zhuǎ)

**Chapter 14**
"势成之" (shì chéng zhī): 作 "孰成之" (shú chéng zhī)

**Chapters 15–16**
"田甚芜" (tián shèn wú): 作 "田甚苗" (tián shèn miáo)

**Chapters 17–18**
"毒虫" (dú chóng): 作 "毒虫" (dú chóng)
"猛兽不复，攫鸟不搏" (měng shòu bù fù, jué niǎo bù bó): 作 "鴝鸟猛兽不搏" (qú niǎo měng shòu bù bó)
"不道" (bù dào): 作 "非道" (fēi dào)
"曰祥" (yuē xiáng): 作 "曰详" (yuē xiáng)

**Chapter 19**
"其纷" (qí fēn): 作 "其忿" (qí fèn)

**Chapter 20**
"以政治国" (yǐ zhèng zhì guó): 作 "以政之国" (yǐ zhèng zhī guó)
"而民自正二" (ér mín zì zhèng èr): 作 "而人自政" (ér rén zì zhèng)

**Chapter 21**
"其民淳淳" (qí mín chún chún): 作 "其人蠢蠢" (qí rén chǔn chǔn)
"祸兮福所倚，福兮祸所伏" (huò xī fú suǒ yǐ, fú xī huò suǒ fú): 作 "祸福之所倚，福祸之所伏" (huò fú zhī suǒ yǐ, fú huò zhī suǒ fú)
"其无正邪，正复为奇，善复为妖" (qí wú zhèng xié, zhèng fù wéi qí, shàn fù wéi yāo): 作 "其无政，政为奇，善复为祆" (qí wú zhèng, zhèng wéi qí, shàn fù wéi xiān)

**Chapter 22**
"莫若啬" (mò ruò sè): 作 "莫若式" (mò ruò shì)
"夫唯啬" (fū wéi sè): 作 "夫唯式" (fū wéi shì)
"则无不克" (zé wú bù kè): 作 "则无不充" (zé wú bù chōng)

**Chapter 23**
"烹小鲜" (pēng xiǎo xiān): 作 "厚小腥" (hòu xiǎo xīng)

**Chapter 24**
"牝牝" (pìn pìn): 作 "牝" (pìn)

**On Stillness as Inferior:** This line originally absent.
*Seizing a great state:* Gathering a great state.
*Thus some lower themselves to seize, some lower themselves and seize:* Thus some lower themselves and seize, some lower themselves and gather.

**Chapter 25**
*What the unvirtuous cling to:* What the unvirtuous do not cling to.
*How could it be discarded:* Why would it be discarded?
*Team of four horses:* Four horses.
*To avoid it:* To avoid.

**Chapter 26**
*Must arise from the minute:* Must arise from the small.

**Chapter 27**
*Its fragility is easily broken:* Its meaning is easily broken.
*A journey of a thousand li begins beneath one’s feet:* A height of a hundred ren rises from beneath one’s feet.
*To remedy the faults of the multitude:* To prepare for the faults of the multitude.

**Chapter 28**
*Intending to delude them:* Intending to amuse them.
*Also a model:* Also serves as a model.

**Chapter 29**
*No contention:* Without contention.

**Chapter 30**
*The world all calls my Dao great, seeming unworthy—precisely because it is great, thus it seems unworthy. Had it seemed worthy for long, it would be small indeed:* The world all deems my greatness unamusing—precisely because it is great, thus it is not amusing. Had it been amusing for long, it would be small.

*I possess three treasures, preserving and holding them:* I possess three treasures, treasuring and holding them.
*Through battle, one triumphs:* Through arraying, one halts.

**Chapters 31–32**
*I dare not act as master:* I dare not seek to be master.
*To treat the enemy lightly:* To slight the enemy.

**Chapter 33**
*Clad in coarse cloth:* Wearing coarse cloth.

**Chapter 34**
*Only because one takes illness as illness, therefore one is not ill:* These two lines originally absent.

**Chapter 35**
*The people do not fear authority:* The people do not fear might.
*Thus the sage:* Therefore the sage.

**Chapter 36**
*Relaxed yet skilled in strategy:* Silent yet skilled in strategy.

**Chapter 37**
*Constantly fearing death:* Constantly unafraid of death.
*Craftsman’s hewing:* Craftsman’s hewing.

The people’s hunger: The people’s hunger.
Because of their excessive pursuit of life: Because of their excessive nurturing of life.

**Chapter 39**
Life is supple and fragile: Life is supple and tender.
Then they unite: Then they embrace.

**Chapter 40**
To offer surplus: Offering surplus.
Not desiring to display worthiness: Thus not valuing worthiness.

**Chapter 41**
None can overcome it: None can precede it.
Therefore the sage: [This line originally absent.]

**Chapter 42**
Governing the tracks: Governing the ruts.

**Chapter 43**
Few among the people possess tools of tenfold or hundredfold: Few possess tools of tenfold or hundredfold.
With nothing to array them: With nothing to deploy them.

**Chapter 44**
[Another version omits "The wise are not broadly learned" and "Goodness is not eloquent" below.]
Having given to others, one possesses even more: Having acted for others, one possesses even more.

Seventy instances of divergence on the right.
Variations in the *De Jing* characters.
