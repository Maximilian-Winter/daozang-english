# 道经异同字

**"Dao Scripture on Heterodox Characters**

**Title:** *Dao Scripture on Heterodox Characters*
**Author:** Anonymous, Tang Dynasty
**Volume:** One scroll
**Source:** *Zhengtong Daozang*, *Dongshen* (Cavernous Gods) Section, *Yujue* (Jade Precepts) Category

---
**Dao Scripture on Heterodox Characters**
*(Verses 1–2)*
*"The existence and nonexistence mutually engender:*
Existence and nonexistence mutually arise.
*All things act without claiming authorship,*
Born without possession,
Working without reliance:*
All things act without beginning,
Born without reliance.

*Thus, the unassuming alone endures:*
Thus, the unoccupied alone endures.

*(Verse 3)*
*Not extolling the virtuous:*
Not elevating the virtuous.
*So that the wise dare not act:*
So that the wise dare not *fail* to act.

*(Verse 4)*
*Or seeming not to overflow:*
Again, not overflowing.
*Dissolving their tumult:*
Dissolving their wrath.
*Deep and ever-present:*
Deep, as if ever-present.

*(Verse 5)*
*Much speech leads to exhaustion:*
Excessive knowledge leads to exhaustion.
*Hold to the central:*
Hold to loyalty.

*(Verse 6)*
*This is called the root of Heaven and Earth:*
Heaven and Earth’s root.

*(Verse 7)*
*Heaven endures, Earth persists:*
Heaven and Earth endure.
*What endures long and persists:*
What endures long.
*Thus, it endures forever:*
Thus, it endures long.

*(Verses 8–9)*
*Long preservation:*
Long treasure.
*Accomplished deed, name fulfilled:*
Name accomplished, deed fulfilled.

*(Verse 10)*
*Each line originally has a ‘hu’ character:*
This text omits the ‘hu’ character.
*‘Heaven’s Gate lies above the Clear’:*
This text places ‘Clear’ above ‘Heaven’s Gate.’

*(Verses 11–13)*
*For my having a body:*
For *my* having a body.

*(Verse 14)*
*Seize it:*
Examine it."

「Shàngshàng: Yíngyíng.  Without form, it is called *hūhuǎng*:
Without form’s likeness.

*(Verse 15)*
If *guì*: If *róng*.
Shall release: Shall *mì*.
Dun, like its *pǔ*: Mixed like *pǔ*.
Kuàng, like its *gǔ*: Vast like *gǔ*.
Hún, like its *zhuó*: Stilled like *zhuó*.
Who can still the turbid to make it gradually clear?
Who can settle the restless to make it gradually flourish?
Thus, it can be worn out yet renewed again:
Thus, it can be worn out and renewed.

*(Verse 16)*
Yúnyún: Yún yún.
The public becomes the king, the thinking becomes the Dao:
The public can generate, generation can become the Dao.

*(Verse 17)*
Yet how much more precious is speech:
Yet how much more precious is speech.

*(Verses 18–20)*
Huāng, like its endless expanse:
Mǎng, like its endless expanse.
Tàiláo: Dàláo.
I alone fear, like its unmanifested sign:
My *pò* is yet unmanifested.
Chéngchéng, like having nowhere to return:
My *kuí* has nowhere to return.
My foolish heart is pure, pure:
My foolish heart is pure.

Hū, like its darkened stillness, seems without end:
Hū, like its darkened stillness, without end.
I alone differ from others:
I desire to differ from others.
Seeking nourishment from the Mother:
Yet valuing nourishment from the Mother.

*(Verse 21)*
Only *hūhuǎng*, *hūhuǎng*—
Within *hūhuǎng*, there is form;
Within *hūhuǎng*, there is substance.
Yǎo, like its profound darkness, within it there is essence;
Its essence is most true, within it there is faith:
Only *yǎo* and *míng*—
Within *yǎo* and *míng*, there is substance,
Within *yǎo* and *míng*, there is form,
Within its profound darkness, there is essence,
Within it, there is faith.

*(Verses 22–23)*
Crooked, then straight:
Crooked, then upright.
Much, then confused:
Much, then *huò*.
Not self-asserting, thus it is manifest:
Not self-asserting, thus it is clear.
Is this not true speech?
Is this not true speech.

Those who follow the Dao:
Dao-followers align with the Dao, virtue-followers align with virtue, those who lose align with loss.
Aligning with the Dao, the Dao is gained;
Aligning with virtue, virtue is gained;
Aligning with loss, the Dao is lost.

*(Verse 24)*」

「To stand on tiptoe is not to stand firm;
To pant is not to endure long.
Self-praise yields no merit;
Self-indulgence yields no merit.
Excessive food and idle movement—
Excessive food, idle conduct.

*(Verse 25)*
The king is great—
The king is vast.

*(Verse 26)*
How can the lord of ten thousand chariots fare?
Lightness leads to losing ministers;
Lightness leads to losing roots.

*(Verse 27)*
Though wise, he is greatly confused.

*(Verse 28)*
The world is a crossroads;
Constant virtue does not falter.
Constant virtue does not fail.

*(Verse 29)*
To act is to ruin it;
Thus, action ruins it.
Sometimes it warms, sometimes it blows;
Sometimes it exhales, sometimes it breathes.
Sometimes it lifts, sometimes it falls;
Sometimes it supports, sometimes it collapses.

*(Verse 30)*
Briars and thorns grow there—
After a great army, there will surely be a year of calamity.
This is called *not-Dao*;
*Not-Dao* has long since passed.

*(Verse 31)*
The left is honored—
The minister is honored.
To eat simply and be content is supreme;
To win yet not boast of victory, yet boast of victory—
This is the delight in killing.
He who delights in killing cannot prevail in the world.
To eat simply and be content is supreme,
Thus, it is not boasted of.
If boasted of, it must be delighted in—
This is the delight in killing.
He who delights in it cannot find joy in the world.

Funeral rites favor the right;
The general of the left is stationed on the left.

*(Verse 32)*
The feudal lords—
The kings and lords.

*(Verses 33–34)*
The Great Dao flows freely—
The Great Dao flows.
When the work is done, it claims no possession;
It nurtures all things yet does not lord over them.

*(Verse 35)*
When the Dao is spoken of,
Its words are bland, without flavor.

*(Verses 36–37)*
The feudal lords—
The kings and lords.

The Dao/Way’s scriptures differ in sixty-nine places.
The texts of the Dao/Way vary in their characters.
