# 白云仙人灵草歌

**The Immortal Baiyun’s Song of Spirit Herbs**

*Scripture Title:* *The Immortal Baiyun’s Song of Spirit Herbs*
*Author:* Anonymous
*Volume:* One
*Source Text:* *Daoist Canon of the Zhengtong Era*, *Section of Divine Spells, Subcategory of Miscellaneous Arts*

---

**The Song of Spirit Herbs**

*(Preface missing)*

The *Dadao* herb bears purple blossoms, with green stems and leaves.
How to refine mercury? Never discard this song of spirit herbs.
Some know only the virtue of red diligence, yet favor the *Dadao* above all.
This herb, singular in form, yields cinnabar grains.

The *Bailu* herb is verdant in hue.
The *Haibao* herb blooms crimson, its stems, calyxes, and leaves all green.
Ten years spent studying immortal arts—few know the spirit herbs.
The *Bailu* serves as a crucible; the *Haibao* softens gold.
Born upon sandy ridges, they are revered by Daoist adepts.
From its crimson core, true mercury is drawn; its red blossoms root deep.
These two herbs alone can soften the five metals.

The *Zizhi* herb bears dark blossoms, its stems, calyxes, and leaves all green.
This herb is not rare—three stems emerge together from the earth.
When cinnabar reveals its white treasure, mercury naturally congeals.
The *Heisui* herb divides at the heart, its blossoms red, tinged with purple, its medicinal stems and leaves green.
The *Shengui* herb, bestowed by heaven, surpasses all others in seeking the *Heisui*.
When the elixir is fired, it is truly praiseworthy, unlike common herbs.
While other herbs wither under frost, this one remains unblemished.
Neither cold nor heat assails it; firing the elixir augments wisdom.
This herb refines the great medicine.

The *Wangxian* herb bears red blossoms and green leaves.
In spring, it carpets the mist like smoke; before ascending, pluck the *Wangxian*.
Subdue cinnabar to make its juice, and mercury will swiftly dry.
The *Jinlian* herb bears red blossoms and green leaves.
For firing the elixir, none is more profound—simply gather the *Jinlian*.
It serves as a crucible; when met by the uninitiated, silence is best.

The *Yuye* herb bears red blossoms and green leaves.
The *Yuye* herb’s redness harmonizes with the great medicine.
The herb’s cinnabar grains reveal its extraordinary efficacy.

The *Dami* herb bears red blossoms and green leaves.
The great Dao demands resolve—remember this herb well.
The Four Spirits submit to it, and it fires the great dual qi.

**Jinfeng Herb**
Black seeds, white roots, green leaves—cherished by immortals like a palm’s treasure.
When true mercury is boiled into hard cinnabar grains, its white body becomes heavy as metal.

**Immortal-Qin Herb**
This herb bears red blossoms and green leaves, with angular seeds.
Growing within great forests, its taste is sweet; it subdues white sulfur.

**Baojian Herb**
This herb grows in three stems, with yellow blossoms and black seeds.
Born upon sandy ground, its taste is bitter, its sap white.
It subdues singular cinnabar to make juice, resisting fire.

**Linquan Herb**
Resembling the daylily, it grows amid great forests.
Red blossoms, green leaves, sweet taste—it subdues singular realgar.

**Diyu Herb**
Verdant in color, it grows beside wastelands.
Astringent in taste, it subdues sal ammoniac.
Boil it with stones and consume—few ailments, body light.

**Jinxian Herb**
This herb grows beside mountain streams.
Yellow blossoms, green leaves, pungent taste.
For all manner of cinnabar grains, wrap them in its blossoms and leaves to resist great fire.

**Dibao Herb**
Slender leaves, straight stems, dark green in hue.
This herb grows near water, its leaves fine, its taste bitter.
It subdues realgar; its juice resists fire, amber in color.

**Datong Herb**
This herb bears red blossoms, green leaves, and black seeds.
Sweet in taste, it grows beside rivers.
With its natural juice, it congeals into cinnabar grains.
The *Datong*’s red blossoms resemble green leaves; its black seeds, white roots, are not pungent.
It grows and nurtures beside rivers, singular in form, yielding true cinnabar grains.

**Tianjiang Herb**
This herb grows in yellow sandy soil.
Green blossoms, verdant leaves, red seeds, pungent taste—it subdues the Four Spirit Elixirs.
These eight secret herbs all congeal cinnabar grains.

The *Zhi* herbs prolong life; the Five Phoenixes form treasures.
The Six Dragons serve as crucibles; the Seven Treasures nourish metal.
The Jade Pillar is fragrant and fine; the Green Spring is bitter and pungent.
The *Wantong* excels at boiling mercury; the *Tianjiang* swiftly refines it.

**Roluo-Yicao Herb**
This herb grows beside forests, pungent in taste.
It subdues powdered frost, resists great fire, and yields superior juice.
Green leaves, five branches—when cinnabar is seen, mercury is boiled in harmony.
Boil mercury into treasure, congealing cinnabar grains from the five metals.
This aligns perfectly with the Dao’s principles.

**Dadao Herb**
This herb grows amid sandy soil.
Sweet in taste, green leaves, white seeds—one blossom yields two large seeds.
It refines cinnabar to draw mercury.
The *Dadao*’s name resounds; yin and yang’s dual qi bear new seeds.
Drawing mercury takes years—who knows where the numinous truth emerges?

*Secret Formula:* The *Dadao* herb has fine leaves; one blossom yields two seeds.
Mercury is naturally renewed; when quicksilver appears, it perishes.

**Changsheng Herb**
This herb grows near water, sweet in taste, with red seeds.
It subdues arsenic.
This herb’s power is rare in the world; few know it without the true text.
Immortals call it the *Changsheng* herb—it excels at firing and subduing arsenic.

**Baihe Herb**
This herb grows within great forests.
Bitter in taste, green leaves—it alone congeals cinnabar grains.
The *Baihe* has its own merit: green leaves, blossoms naturally red.
It subdues true mercury, dwelling long within the forest.

**Moluo Herb**
Green stems, verdant leaves, purple blossoms, abundant white sap.
The great Dao names it *Moluo*.
It prolongs life and serves many crucibles.

**Wuyun Herb**
The *Fengqing* herb bears red blossoms and green leaves.
To ascend in the Dao, one must seek the scripture of spirit herbs.
Sacred matters should always be revered; do not lightly share them with the unworthy.
Lest one inquire about longevity, it appears as a treasure in the palm.
To master metal and mercury, gather the *Fengqing* herb.

**Xianzhi Herb**
This herb grows within great forests.
Sweet in taste, yellow blossoms, black seeds, green leaves—its juice boils true cinnabar mercury.
The *Xianzhi* is truly praiseworthy, blooming yellow amidst snow.

**Cold and Heat Unobstructed Herb**
Cold and heat do not hinder each other; this solitary herb congeals cinnabar.
The **Jade Pillar Herb** bears azure blossoms and verdant leaves.
Grind this herb to obtain its natural juice, blend evenly with sulfur—whether dry or moist—and place within a Dingzhou crucible. Nurture with gentle fire until red, then refine with great fire for one night. It yields white sulfur, resisting fire to form a molten essence.

**Bright Moon Herb**
The **Water Red Herb** bears red blossoms, is pure in nature, with crimson stems.
Heaven names it *Water Red*; its intent to subdue mercury is not weighty.
Yet it uniquely subdues white sal ammoniac and serves the great crucibles of yin and yang.
This herb may also be ingested, greatly treating diseases of water qi.

**Red Flame Herb**
This herb grows within water, bearing red blossoms, green leaves, and a pungent taste.
It is said that herbs congealing cinnabar, as well as all stones forming cinnabar, when placed within the **Red Flame Herb**, are fully subdued without loss.

**Earth Banana Herb**
Red blossoms, black seeds, green stems and leaves.
Its power to subdue medicines is unmatched in clarity and excellence—none surpass the **Earth Banana**.
Not only does it draw forth true mercury, but it also subdues saltpeter within stones.
The **Earth Banana** subdues saltpeter and extracts mercury.

**Treasure Mountain Herb**
Red blossoms, green leaves.
This numinous herb is called *Treasure Mountain*, dwelling within great forests.
Immortal families guard it in secret; its worth is ten thousand coins.
Its juice boils mercury into hardened cinnabar, also subduing and revealing treasure cinnabar.

**Lesser White Fortune Herb**
Red blossoms, stems, calyxes, and leaves all green.
This herb bears red blossoms and green leaves, growing upon high ridges. Its taste is pungent.
Whenever one seeks to subdue medicines that fly or run among men—such as medicinal pastes, powder, or mercury—as well as ingested medicines, all are refined without loss when burned.

**Lesser Gold Thread Herb**
Red blossoms, green leaves.
This numinous herb shines brightly; the **Gold Thread**’s strength is most steadfast.
It is indeed rare cinnabar, naturally drying when wrapped in its leaves.
Its taste is pungent, growing within great forests. It governs great medicines and activates mercury cinnabar. Simply wrap it in fire, and it becomes a treasure.

**True Pearl Herb**
Numinous sprouts subdue medicines well—none surpass the **True Pearl Herb**.
Subduing medicines brings no worry; those who ingest it do not age.
Its juice is sweet in taste, bearing yellow blossoms. It grows within deep forests; those who encounter it live long without illness.

**Golden Simurgh Herb**
The great Dao conceals the **Golden Simurgh**; even immortal families do not transmit it.
It is fit to subdue mercury and lead, surpassing even the crucible of *Yongquan*.
This herb grows along riverbanks, bearing yellow blossoms and green leaves. It subdues and governs mercury and lead, worthy of the *Yongquan* crucible.

**Sea Pomegranate Herb**
Red blossoms, green leaves.
Seeking mercury knows no rest—none surpass the **Sea Pomegranate**.
Without encountering this numinous herb’s root, one’s life nears its end!
Its taste is bitter, yet the seeds it bears are sweet. When alive, mercury clings to its juice and withers—thus it is also called the *Withered Mercury Herb*.

**Red Indigo Herb**
The **Assembled Treasure Herb** has green stems and leaves.
Heaven bestows the name *Assembled Treasure*; its leaves remain verdant through all four seasons.
Most people do not recognize it, though it resembles gold within the palm.
Ingesting it lengthens life; consuming it raw effectively treats the heart.
Were this not the true immortal’s root, few in the world would know its melody.
This herb grows upon sandy ridges. Its taste is sweet and can refine all medicines without loss.

**Sea Peach Herb**
Numinous branches are called **Sea Peach**; ingesting it effectively treats consumptive illness.
Not only does it lengthen life, but it also softens mercury and tin.
This herb grows within great forests, greatly treating consumptive diseases. It bears yellow blossoms and green leaves, with a bitter taste, and can soften the five metals.

**Golden Lamp Herb**
This herb’s taste is pungent; immortal families call it **Golden Lamp**.
Ingesting it cures illness; after one year, the body becomes light.
It whitens *pīpī* and congeals mercury, renewing it.
Otherwise, it saves lives and extracts lead from within mercury.
This herb’s taste is pungent, growing upon high slopes. It bears purple blossoms and green leaves, drawing forth mercury and subduing lead.

**Mountain Azure Herb**
The immortal herb is named **Mountain Azure**; its roots resemble Solomon’s seal.
Those who ingest it find their hair blackened again; it especially cures fright in children.
Burning it serves as a crucible; its leaves remain ever-new through summer and autumn.
This herb grows within high forests, bearing red blossoms and azure leaves. Its taste is sweet and subdues sulfur.

**Ten-Thousand-Penetrating Herb**
The great Dao follows immortal traces, only then revealing its divine efficacy.
The Three Yellows form the true body; the White Tiger is of one lineage.
Seeking the golden elixir through numinous herbs, the stone crucible’s depths are swiftly fathomed.
To master lead and mercury, one must gather the **Ten-Thousand-Penetrating Herb**.
This herb grows upon white sandy ground. Its taste is astringent, bearing azure blossoms and green leaves. Ingesting it grants long life without illness, uniquely subduing great medicines without loss.

**White Pearl Herb**
Yellow blossoms, green leaves.
The great Dao and immortals share the same path—one must seek merit among herbs.
Metals and stones may fly and flee, but numinous herbs naturally commune.
Simply rely on true immortal herbs to govern within lead and mercury.
To achieve the golden elixir, gather the **Mountain Red Herb**.
This herb grows within high ridges or upon earthen mountains. It bears red blossoms, green leaves, and a sweet taste. Those who ingest it live a thousand years.

**Dragon Spring Herb**
All things sprout in spring’s advent, yet only the **Dragon Spring** emerges from the earth first.
When encountering treasure cinnabar, wrap it in its leaves; its natural juice boils mercury into dryness.
This herb grows within deep grasses, bearing azure blossoms and black seeds. Those who ingest it become light of body and free from illness.

**Treasure Peak Herb**
The great Dao dwells within the bustling market; to seek the Dao, do not turn west or east.

Seek only true lead and mercury, for the four matters all interconnect.
The sagely work follows the teachings, never straying from the great immortal’s path.
To know when the **Yellow Sprout** appears, gather the **Treasure Peak Herb**.
This herb bears red blossoms, green leaves, and black seeds, with a pungent taste. Those who ingest it live free from illness, light of body, and with enduring youth.
It also guards the great medicine, skillfully yielding the **Yellow Sprout**.

**Gold Coin Herb**
To attain the Dao and prolong life, nothing surpasses seeking the **Gold Coin Herb**.
Ingesting it lengthens one’s years, dries mercury, and preserves youthful countenance.
This herb grows within deep grasses. Those who ingest it remain free from illness. It has a sweet taste, yellow blossoms, white juice, green leaves, and forms cinnabar grains alone.

**Immortal Maiden Herb**
The transformations of the **Purple River Chariot** cannot compare to the **Immortal Maiden Herb**.
The flying, flowing medicine—how the great Dao knows it!
This herb grows in green sandy soil, bearing yellow blossoms and a sweet taste. It subdues all medicines without fail.

**Yellow Sprout Herb**
Its blossoms and leaves remain verdant, its roots white as gold.
The **Yellow Sprout** grows in barren earth—how many recognize it?

The root of this herb excels in treating toothache.
**Green Gold Herb** bears yellow blossoms and green leaves.
Long has it been heard that wondrous herbs exist—yet none surpass **Green Gold**.
Its tender branches, when ingested, keep one from aging.
This herb grows by waterside, uniquely healing malignant sores.

**Seven Stars Herb**
This herb grows within renowned mountains. Those who encounter and ingest it may attain deathlessness and prolonged years.
Its blossoms are green, its leaves red, its taste sweet, and it subdues great medicines.

**Mountain Sap Herb**
**Mountain Sap Herb** has a sour taste and red blossoms. It grows in yellow sandy soil, uniquely curing human malaria, and restrains sulfur.

**Silver Thread Herb**
**Silver Thread Herb** bears red blossoms, black seeds, and green stems and leaves.
This herb grows within renowned mountains, greatly curing wind-borne illnesses. Its blossoms are red, its seeds black, its taste sweet, and it subdues sal ammoniac.

**Fitting-for-Sons Herb**
**Fitting-for-Sons Herb** has red blossoms, green leaves, and green calyxes.
This herb grows within renowned mountains. Those who chance upon and ingest it shall live five hundred years.
Its taste is sweet, its blossoms red, its seeds green, and its leaves verdant.

**Golden Net Herb**
**Golden Net Herb** bears yellow blossoms and green branches and leaves.
This herb grows beneath pine trees. Its blossoms are yellow, its taste pungent. Those who encounter and ingest it shall not die for a thousand years, and it subdues great medicines.

**Carambola Herb**
**Carambola Herb** has purple thorns and green foliage.
**Carambola Herb** exudes white sap, growing upon thorny brambles. It subdues cinnabar and greatly heals malignant sores.

**Hundred Gold Herb**
**Hundred Gold Herb** bears red blossoms and green branches and leaves.
This herb grows within great forests. Its blossoms are red, its taste sweet, and it greatly subdues true mercury.

Secret Saying:
Seventy-two herbs all possess numinous power,
Each subduing cinnabar and communing with spirits.
Were they not left behind by the upper realms,
Mortals below would seldom hear of them.

*White Cloud Immortal’s Song of Numinous Herbs* concludes.
