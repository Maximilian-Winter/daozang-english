*Title:* *Direct Phonetic Guide to the True Scripture of Southern Florescence*
Compiled by Jia Shanxiang of the Northern Song, completed in the bingyin year of the Yuanyou era. One fascicle.
Source of the base text: *Daoist Canon of the Zhengtong Era*, *Wondrous Writings* section, *Jade Instructions* category.

*Direct Phonetic Guide to the True Scripture of Southern Florescence*
Respectfully presented by Disciple Jia Shanxiang, Perfected Master of Profound Awakening

From youth, when the literati of the world first bind their hair to study, they select teachers and friends to receive instruction. Thus they engage in lofty discourse and profound debate, discern truth from falsehood, and thereafter enjoy honor and wealth, their reputations spreading boundlessly—all beginning from this. This is what is meant by "for even a single fascicle of a book, one must establish a teacher."

Yet those in the world who delight in such matters often neglect to choose teachers or companions. Instead, they casually peruse texts at leisure to suit their dispositions. However, when they encounter obscure characters, punctuation, or borrowed usages, they frequently fail to recognize them. They then consult phonetic glossaries, but such glosses, with their complex fanqie systems, remain inscrutable. After checking once, twice, or thrice, their minds grow weary, and unawares, they close the book and retire. If one does not recognize the characters, one cannot grasp the meaning; without meaning, there is no flavor; without flavor, no delight; and without delight, how can weariness be avoided?

I have not merely heard of this from acquaintances but have witnessed it firsthand. Thus, in moments of quiet reflection, I have taken the abstruse characters in the works of Laozi and Zhuangzi—along with punctuation and borrowed usages—and annotated them with simpler graphs. For those difficult to explicate with common characters, I have provided phonetic approximations. This is to ensure that readers may easily identify the words. I have titled it *Direct Phonetic Guide*, offering a modest aid to scholars in one small respect.

Recorded in the winter of the bingyin year of the Yuanyou era by the Recluse of Pengqiu.
