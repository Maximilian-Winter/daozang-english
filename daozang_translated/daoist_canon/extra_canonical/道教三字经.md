# 道教三字经

**"Daoist Three-Character Classic" by Yi Xinying**

**I: The Dao’s Single Qi Transforms the Three Pure Ones**
The Great Dao, one in *qi*, gives birth to the Three Pure Ones.
Most void and numinous, most subtle and profound.
Though named with force, it is the Great Dao.
Its essence is naturally so—
Manifest in Oneness, it precedes the Sovereign’s form.
Formless and boundless, its essence is profound.
From its mysterious transformation, light and sound arise.
It cleaves the murky chaos, gradually brightening.
The Boundless shifts, dividing into three *qi*s.
First, the Azure *qi*—named *Qingwei*.
The Dragon-Han kalpa shines with celestial splendor.
The Primordial White *qi*—named *Yuyu*.
The True Text appears, illuminating the Great Void.
The Dark-Yellow *qi*—named *Dachi*.
The Sovereign Above opens, and myriad transformations teem.

A sacred platform is erected within the Treasure Pearl.
Scriptures are transmitted, their meanings boundless.
Our august sovereign gathers the Cloud Books,
Correcting the Celestial Chant at the kalpa’s dawn.
He translates the Three Caverns, then the Four Auxiliaries—
Seventy-two in total, surpassing all ages.

**III: The Gods Proclaim the Dao and Ascend to Heaven**

He transforms kings, saving subjects.

They receive this decree and ascend in broad daylight.

**IV: The Ancient Daoist Lineage Venerates the Great Cavern Scripture**

In the Pure Joy Realm, a prince emerged,
Free from dust, liberated from birth and death.
Entering the Great Harmony, he toiled diligently.
Brandishing the Wisdom Sword, he subdued the Two Tyrants.
Leaping from the Southern Cliff, he manifested True Martial.
His great deed accomplished, he repaid his parents.
King Fu Cha of Wu revered him deeply.
Seeking to save others, he went to Qi.
The *Kongdong Pian* was not lightly revealed.
This tradition, passed down for forty thousand kalpas,
Is one lineage, venerating the Great Cavern.
Researching the True Principle, Canghu was weighty.
